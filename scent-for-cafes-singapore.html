What is scent marketing for a café?

Scent marketing is the deliberate use of a signature ambient scent to shape how customers feel and behave in your space. For a café it does three things: it pulls passers-by in (craving), it makes people linger and order more (comfort), and it leaves a smell they associate with your brand long after they leave (memory). Unlike a discount or a new sign, scent works on the part of the brain wired straight to emotion and memory — before conscious thought.

Does scent marketing work for cafés?

The research is consistent across retail and hospitality:

  • Customers in a scented store spent about 20% more (Journal of Retailing).
  • 84% preferred a product shown in a scented room (Spangenberg, Washington State University).
  • Ambient-scent studies show sales lifts of 3–23% (2020 meta-analysis), and industry reports put typical in-store lift around 10%, with longer dwell time.

For a café, dwell time matters as much as footfall — the longer someone stays, the more likely a second coffee or a slice of cake follows.

What scents suit a coffee or dessert café?

Cafés have it slightly easier than bubble tea shops — hot coffee already gives off aroma — but most of that smell stays behind the counter and fades fast. A tuned signature scent makes it consistent and front-of-house. Strong choices:

  • Roasted coffee — deepens your core identity and reads instantly.
  • Vanilla & caramel — warm, comforting, and they make sweet items taste sweeter, which can support lower-sugar recipes.
  • Chocolate, brown sugar, toasted nut — for dessert-led cafés.
